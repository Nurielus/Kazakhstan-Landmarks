Charyn Canyon is often called the "younger brother" of the Grand Canyon. It is a unique natural formation with breathtaking rock formations.

Charyn Canyon, located in southeastern Kazakhstan, is often compared to the Grand Canyon due to its dramatic rock formations and colorful landscapes. Spanning approximately 154 kilometers, it features towering cliffs, deep gorges, and unique rock structures shaped by erosion over millions of years. The most famous part, the "Valley of Castles," showcases surreal sandstone formations resembling ancient fortresses. Visitors can hike, camp, or explore the canyon’s diverse ecosystems. With its stunning scenery and geological wonders, Charyn Canyon is one of Kazakhstan’s most remarkable natural attractions, drawing adventurers and photographers from around the world.
